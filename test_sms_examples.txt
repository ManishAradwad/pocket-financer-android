================================================================================
POCKET FINANCER - SMS TEST EXAMPLES
================================================================================

This file contains examples of long, valid transactional SMS messages, along with
their corresponding ADB injection commands. Run these commands in PowerShell to
inject them directly into emulator-5554.

First, ensure emulator shell WRITE_SMS permission is allowed:
& "C:\Users\manis\AppData\Local\Android\Sdk\platform-tools\adb.exe" -s emulator-5554 shell appops set com.android.shell WRITE_SMS allow

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Example 1: High-Value Credit Card Spend (VK-AMEXIN)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Sender: VK-AMEXIN
SMS Body:
Alert: You have spent INR 89,450.75 on your American Express Card ending XX9876 at MARRIOTT HOTELS RESORTS MUMBAI on 24-06-2026 19:15:30. The transaction has been approved. Your outstanding balance is INR 1,42,390.40 and your available credit limit is INR 3,57,609.60. If you did not make this transaction or notice any suspicious activity, please call the Amex customer helpline immediately at 1800-419-2122 or visit americanexpress.co.in/report-fraud to secure your card.

ADB Injection Command (PowerShell):
$timestamp = [DateTimeOffset]::Now.ToUnixTimeMilliseconds()
& "C:\Users\manis\AppData\Local\Android\Sdk\platform-tools\adb.exe" -s emulator-5554 shell content insert --uri content://sms/inbox --bind address:s:'VK-AMEXIN' --bind body:s:'Alert: You have spent INR 89,450.75 on your American Express Card ending XX9876 at MARRIOTT HOTELS RESORTS MUMBAI on 24-06-2026 19:15:30. The transaction has been approved. Your outstanding balance is INR 1,42,390.40 and your available credit limit is INR 3,57,609.60. If you did not make this transaction or notice any suspicious activity, please call the Amex customer helpline immediately at 1800-419-2122 or visit americanexpress.co.in/report-fraud to secure your card.' --bind date:l:$timestamp --bind read:i:1
